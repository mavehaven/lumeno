Wave Interference Lab $y = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$
Wave equation
$$\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial t^2} = v^2 \frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial x^2}$$

Describes how displacement propagates through a medium at phase speed v.

Dispersion relation
$$v = f\lambda = \frac{\omega}{k}$$

Links wave speed, frequency f, wavelength λ, angular frequency ω and wavenumber k.

Superposition
$$y_{\text{total}} = \sum_i A_i \sin(k_i x - \omega_i t + \phi_i)$$

Multiple waves add linearly. Interference patterns emerge from phase differences.
