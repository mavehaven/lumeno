Wave Interference Lab $y = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$
Wave equation
$$\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial t^2} = v^2 \frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial x^2}$$

Describes how displacement propagates through a medium at phase speed v.

Dispersion relation
$$v = f\lambda = \frac{\omega}{k}$$

Links wave speed, frequency f, wavelength λ, angular frequency ω and wavenumber k.

Superposition
$$y_{\text{total}} = \sum_i A_i \sin(k_i x - \omega_i t + \phi_i)$$

Multiple waves add linearly. Interference patterns emerge from phase differences.

How is LumenOS different from YouTube or Khan Academy?+
LumenOS is interactive, not passive. You control the variables, make predictions, and see results in real time. Watching a video of a pendulum is not the same as dragging its length and feeling why the period changes.
Do I need any prior physics or maths knowledge?+
No. Simulations are structured progressively. You start with intuitive exploration and the equations appear naturally as you discover them. We guide you from "I have no idea what's happening" to "I could teach this."
What are the private lessons?+
Private lessons are one-on-one live sessions with a LumenOS tutor — a subject-matter expert who guides you through a specific concept, walks you through a simulation, or helps you prepare for an exam. Student plan includes 2 sessions per month; Pro plan includes unlimited sessions.
Are the certificates recognised by universities or employers?+
Our badges are verifiable and shareable on LinkedIn. We are actively building employer partnerships — Pro plan users gain direct access to our opportunity board where verified skills are matched to roles and internships.
Can I cancel my subscription anytime?+
Yes, always. No lock-in, no questions asked. Your progress and certificates remain accessible on the Free plan after cancellation.
Is there a student discount?+
The Student plan is already priced for students. If your institution has a bulk license, contact us — we offer group pricing that brings costs down significantly for universities and schools.
