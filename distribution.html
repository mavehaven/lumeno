01 — Foundations

The Mathematical Bedrock

Probability spaces, random variables, and the language every distribution is built on

Kolmogorov's Probability Space

Every probability distribution lives inside a triple \((\Omega,\mathcal{F},P)\).

AXIOM I — NON-NEGATIVITY
\[P(A)\geq 0\quad\forall\,A\in\mathcal{F}\]
AXIOM II — NORMALIZATION
\[P(\Omega)=1\]
AXIOM III — COUNTABLE ADDITIVITY
\[P\!\left(\bigcup_{i=1}^{\infty}A_i\right)=\sum_{i=1}^{\infty}P(A_i)\]

Sample Space Ω

All possible outcomes. Discrete: \(\Omega=\{1,\ldots,6\}\). Continuous: \(\Omega=\mathbb{R}\).

Sigma-Algebra ℱ

Subsets closed under complement & countable union. Defines which events are measurable.

Random Variable X

A measurable function \(X:\Omega\to\mathbb{R}\). Maps outcomes to numbers we can compute with.

CDF \(F_X\)

\(F_X(x)=P(X\leq x)\). Right-continuous, non-decreasing, \(F(-\infty)=0\), \(F(+\infty)=1\).

Key derived results: \(P(A^c)=1-P(A)\),  \(P(\emptyset)=0\),  \(P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)\),  \(A\subseteq B\Rightarrow P(A)\leq P(B)\).
Discrete

Probability Mass Function

PMF & Normalization
\[p_X(x)=P(X=x),\quad\sum_{x\in\mathcal{X}}p_X(x)=1\]

Range probabilities: \(P(a\leq X\leq b)=\sum_{k=a}^{b}p_X(k)\).

Examples: Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Geometric, Hypergeometric, Negative Binomial.

Continuous

Probability Density Function

PDF & Normalization
\[f_X(x)\geq 0,\quad\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}f_X(x)\,dx=1\]

\(P(a\leq X\leq b)=\int_a^b f_X(x)\,dx\). Note: \(P(X=x)=0\) for any single point.

Examples: Normal, Exponential, Beta, Gamma, Uniform, Cauchy, Student-t, Weibull.

CDF — The Universal Connector
Fundamental Theorem of Probability
\[F_X(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{x}f_X(t)\,dt\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad f_X(x)=\frac{d}{dx}F_X(x)\]

The lime curve shows the PDF of \(\mathcal{N}(0,1)\); the cyan curve shows its CDF — integrated area from \(-\infty\) to \(x\).

The Moment Generating Machine
n-TH RAW MOMENT
\[\mu'_n=E[X^n]=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x^n f(x)\,dx\]
n-TH CENTRAL MOMENT
\[\mu_n=E[(X-\mu)^n]=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(x-\mu)^n f(x)\,dx\]
MOMENT GENERATING FUNCTION
\[M_X(t)=E[e^{tX}],\quad\mu'_n=M_X^{(n)}(0)\]
CHARACTERISTIC FUNCTION (exists for ALL distributions)
\[\varphi_X(t)=E[e^{itX}]=\int e^{itx}f(x)\,dx\]

Mean — 1st moment

\(\mu=E[X]\) — center of mass of the distribution. Minimizes \(E[(X-c)^2]\).

Variance — 2nd central

\(\sigma^2=E[(X-\mu)^2]=E[X^2]-\mu^2\). Always \(\geq 0\).

Skewness — 3rd standardized

\(\gamma_1=E\!\left[\!\left(\tfrac{X-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^3\right]\). Positive = right tail, negative = left tail.

Excess Kurtosis — 4th

\(\gamma_2=E\!\left[\!\left(\tfrac{X-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^4\right]-3\). Positive = heavier tails than Normal.

Cumulants: \(\kappa_1=\mu\), \(\kappa_2=\sigma^2\), \(\kappa_3=\mu_3\). Cumulants of the sum of independent RVs add: \(\kappa_n(X+Y)=\kappa_n(X)+\kappa_n(Y)\). This is why the Normal (all cumulants \(\kappa_{n\geq3}=0\)) is the attractor of the CLT.
Modes of Convergence — Strongest to Weakest
ALMOST SURE (a.s.) — STRONGEST
\[P\!\left(\lim_{n\to\infty}X_n=X\right)=1\]
CONVERGENCE IN L² (MEAN SQUARE)
\[\lim_{n\to\infty}E\!\left[(X_n-X)^2\right]=0\]
CONVERGENCE IN PROBABILITY
\[\forall\,\varepsilon>0:\;\lim_{n\to\infty}P(|X_n-X|>\varepsilon)=0\]
CONVERGENCE IN DISTRIBUTION — WEAKEST
\[\lim_{n\to\infty}F_{X_n}(x)=F_X(x)\quad\text{at all continuity points }x\]
Implications: a.s. ⟹ probability ⟹ distribution. Also L² ⟹ probability ⟹ distribution. The CLT is a convergence-in-distribution result. The Strong LLN is an almost-sure result.
Cauchy has no finite mean or variance. Both LLN and CLT fail. The sample mean of \(n\) Cauchy variables is itself Cauchy — it never concentrates regardless of \(n\).
Chebyshev's Inequality — Live Explorer
CHEBYSHEV'S INEQUALITY
\[P(|X-\mu|\geq k\sigma)\leq\frac{1}{k^2}\quad\forall\,k>1\]

Chebyshev's bound is distribution-free — it holds for any \(X\) with finite mean and variance. The actual probability for the Normal is far smaller.

Fundamental Limit Theorems

Two of the most profound results in all of mathematics — emerging live from raw randomness

Central Limit Theorem (Lindeberg–Lévy)
\[\sqrt{n}\,\frac{\bar{X}_n-\mu}{\sigma}\;\xrightarrow{d}\;\mathcal{N}(0,1)\quad\text{as }n\to\infty\]

i.i.d. variables \(X_i\) with finite mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2\). The red curve is \(\mathcal{N}(0,1)\); the histogram is empirical standardized means.

Weak Law (Chebyshev 1867)
\[\bar{X}_n\xrightarrow{P}\mu\iff\lim_{n\to\infty}P(|\bar{X}_n-\mu|>\varepsilon)=0\]
Strong Law (Borel 1909)
\[\bar{X}_n\xrightarrow{a.s.}\mu\iff P\!\left(\lim_{n\to\infty}\bar{X}_n=\mu\right)=1\]
