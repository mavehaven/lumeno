2D Transformations

2D Linear Transformations

A linear transformation \( T: \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2 \) is a function that satisfies:

  • \( T(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}) = T(\mathbf{u}) + T(\mathbf{v}) \) for all vectors \( \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \)
  • \( T(c\mathbf{v}) = cT(\mathbf{v}) \) for all vectors \( \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \) and scalars \( c \in \mathbb{R} \)

Every linear transformation can be represented by a 2×2 matrix \( A \) such that:

\( T(\mathbf{v}) = A\mathbf{v} \)

Where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \)

Common 2D Transformations

Scaling: \( \begin{bmatrix} s_x & 0 \\ 0 & s_y \end{bmatrix} \)

Rotation: \( \begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix} \)

Shear: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & k \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) (horizontal) or \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ k & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) (vertical)

Reflection: \( \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) (across y-axis) or \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \) (across x-axis)

Properties

  • Preserves vector addition and scalar multiplication
  • Maps lines to lines
  • Preserves the origin (0,0)
  • The determinant represents how area scales under the transformation

3D Transformations

3D Linear Transformations

A linear transformation \( T: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3 \) satisfies:

  • \( T(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}) = T(\mathbf{u}) + T(\mathbf{v}) \) for all vectors \( \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^3 \)
  • \( T(c\mathbf{v}) = cT(\mathbf{v}) \) for all vectors \( \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^3 \) and scalars \( c \in \mathbb{R} \)

Every 3D linear transformation can be represented by a 3×3 matrix \( A \) such that:

\( T(\mathbf{v}) = A\mathbf{v} \)

Where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} \)

Common 3D Transformations

Scaling: \( \begin{bmatrix} s_x & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & s_y & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & s_z \end{bmatrix} \)

Rotation around X-axis: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ 0 & \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix} \)

Rotation around Y-axis: \( \begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & 0 & \sin\theta \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin\theta & 0 & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix} \)

Rotation around Z-axis: \( \begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta & 0 \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)

Properties

  • Preserves vector addition and scalar multiplication
  • Maps lines to lines and planes to planes
  • Preserves the origin (0,0,0)
  • The determinant represents how volume scales under the transformation
  • Orthogonal transformations (rotations, reflections) preserve lengths and angles

Current 3D Transformation

The transformation matrix is applied to all points in 3D space. The determinant of this matrix is 1.00.

T(x, y, z) = [1 0 0; 0 1 0; 0 0 1] · [x; y; z]
