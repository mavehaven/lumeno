Theory & Mathematical Explanation

The light field effect is achieved by computing the depth angle between a fixed reference point (the "Sweet Spot") and the current mouse position (or an auto-rotating angle). An oscillating shift is applied to multiple overlaid samples of the image, creating a dynamic parallax effect.

The oscillating amplitude is computed as:

$$ \alpha(t) = A \sin(\omega t) $$

The shift applied for each sample is given by:

$$ S(\theta) = \alpha(t) \cdot \Delta \cdot \cos(\theta - \theta_0) $$

where:

  • \(A\) is the maximum amplitude (set by the Amplitude slider),
  • \(\omega\) is the angular frequency (controlled by the Animation Speed slider),
  • \(\Delta\) is the product of the Spatial Resolution and the Depth Cone multiplier,
  • \(\theta\) represents the sample’s angular position, and
  • \(\theta_0\) is the computed depth angle between the Sweet Spot and the mouse (or auto angle).
